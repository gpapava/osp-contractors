Who We Are

About OSP Contractors

OSP Contractors was established in 2014, representing the natural evolution of founder Apostolos Mpesiris's prior enterprise — a company that spent over four decades undertaking utilities infrastructure projects, with particular specialisation in telecommunications.

The name OSP stands for Outside Plant — all the physical cabling and supporting infrastructure (such as conduit, cabinets, towers or poles), along with any associated hardware (such as repeaters) located between a demarcation point in a switching facility and a demarcation point in another switching centre or customer premises.

Our highest objective is to fully satisfy client requirements and to deliver superior-quality services. We achieve this by leveraging our own internal company resources at all times — we never use subcontractors.

Self-Sufficient

OSP Contractors supports its own dedicated crews across every discipline:

Our History

Key Milestones

  1. 1981

    Predecessor Founded

    Apostolos Mpesiris establishes a utilities infrastructure company, beginning over four decades of telecom specialisation across Greece.

  2. 2012

    Significant Project Activity Begins

    Large-scale copper cable recovery and business customer ad-hoc projects across Northern Greece commence.

  3. 2014

    OSP Contractors Established

    The company is formally incorporated, inheriting the expertise and client relationships of the predecessor organisation. NGA FTTC network development begins across Greece.

  4. 2017

    Pilot Projects

    Pilot FTTH deployment in Rhodes and Smart City project in Trikala demonstrate the company's capability for complex, cutting-edge deployments.

  5. 2018

    FTTH Rollout Across Greece

    Large-scale FTTH network deployments begin, spanning all regions of Greece and delivering hundreds of kilometres of fibre infrastructure.

  6. Today

    Continuing to Grow

    With 100+ professionals, offices in Athens and Trikala, and an expanding portfolio of completed projects, OSP Contractors continues to shape Greece's telecom infrastructure.
